About

Dr. M. K. Jaganathan

Dr M. K. Jaganathan is an Assistant Professor (Senior Grade) in the Department of Biotechnology at SRM Institute of Science and Technology (SRMIST), Kattankulathur, India. With nearly two decades of experience in teaching, research, and academic mentoring, he is committed to advancing biotechnology through interdisciplinary research that integrates environmental science, molecular biology, pharmaceutical biotechnology, and bioinformatics.

Dr. Jaganathan's research focuses on understanding the biological effects of environmental contaminants, particularly endocrine-disrupting chemicals and emerging pollutants, using advanced experimental models and molecular techniques. His work spans environmental toxicology, reproductive toxicology, pharmaceutical biotechnology, marine biotechnology, nanomedicine, computational biology, and AI-driven drug discovery, aiming to develop innovative solutions for human health and environmental sustainability.

He has published research articles in reputed international journals and actively contributes to scientific advancements through collaborative research, peer review, and student mentorship. His laboratory is engaged in multidisciplinary projects involving natural products, nanotechnology-based therapeutics, molecular toxicology, and network pharmacology, combining experimental and computational methods to address complex biomedical challenges.

As an educator, Dr. Jaganathan is passionate about creating an engaging learning environment that encourages critical thinking, innovation, and research excellence. He has guided numerous undergraduate and postgraduate students in biotechnology and actively supervises research projects in areas ranging from environmental toxicology to artificial intelligence applications in life sciences.

Beyond research and teaching, Dr. Jaganathan is involved in curriculum development, outcome-based education, laboratory modernization, and academic quality initiatives. His vision is to bridge the gap between biological sciences and emerging digital technologies, fostering the next generation of researchers capable of addressing global healthcare and environmental challenges through interdisciplinary science.

His long-term research mission is to develop sustainable biotechnological solutions by integrating experimental biology, computational intelligence, and translational research to improve human health, protect ecosystems, and contribute to scientific innovation.
