Total RNA Isolation

Aim Principle Materials Procedure and Simulation Precautions

Aim

To isolate total RNA from the given bacterial culture.

Principle

Total RNA is isolated and separated from DNA and protein after extraction with a solution called as Trizol (Sigma). Trizol is an acidic solution containing guanidinium thiocyanate (GITC), phenol and chloroform. GITC irreversibly denatures proteins and RNase. This is followed by centrifugation. Under acidic conditions, total RNA remains in the upper aqueous phase, while most of DNA and proteins remain either in the interphase or in the lower organic phase. Total RNA is then recovered by precipitation with isopropanol. RNase enzymes can be inactivated by including diethyl pyrrocarbonate (DEPC).

Materials Required

  • Luria Broth
  • Bacterial culture containing plasmid
  • Trizol
  • TAE Buffer
  • Chloroform
  • Isopropanol
  • 70% Ethanol
  • Autoclaved Distilled Water
  • Microcentrifuge tubes
  • Micropipettes and Tips
  • Microcentrifuge

Procedure

  1. Take 800 µL of bacterial culture in a fresh eppendorf. To this add 160 µL of Trizol (1/5th of culture volume).
  2. The solution was mixed well by pipetting several times. To this add 32 µl of chloroform (1/5th volume of trizol).
  3. Incubate for 2 to 5 minutes and centrifuge at 12000 rpm for 15 minutes at 4° C.
  4. Transfer the aqueous phase into a new tube and add equal volume of isopropanol. Mix well.
  5. Centrifuge at 10000 rpm for 10 minutes at 4° C.
  6. Discard the supernatant and resuspend the pellet in 70% ethanol.
  7. Again centrifuge at 10000 rpm for 10 minutes at 4° C.
  8. Discard the supernatant. Air dry the pellet at 37° C for 10-15 minutes.
  9. Resuspend the pellet in 50 µL of TE buffer.
  10. Analyse the RNA sample quantitatively (Spectrophotometry) and qualitatively (Agarose gel).

Precautions

  • Use DNase-free tubes and tips.
  • Do not vortex after cell lysis.
  • Wash pellet with 70% ethanol.
